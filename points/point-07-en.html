Historical context

The Prefecture Palace, located at 8 Via Torquato Tasso, during the Fascist Ventennio was the seat of the highest local authority of the State. The Prefect represented the central Government and exercised strong political and administrative control over the territory.

During the Italian Social Republic, the prefectorial office was transformed into that of Head of the Province, further strengthening the regime‚Äôs repressive role at the local level.

Why it is a place of memory

This building is linked to key moments of the Bergamo Resistance and the Liberation. On 8 September 1943, on the eve of the German occupation, Ettore Tulli organized an operation here to recover weapons, giving rise to the first arsenal of the partisan group ‚ÄúPisacane.‚Äù

On 26 April 1945, the Prefecture was the first building to be liberated by the partisans. The National Liberation Committee appointed Ezio Zambianchi as Prefect of the Liberation, marking the transition from fascist power to democratic authority.

In-depth study ‚Äì Ettore Tulli and the ‚ÄúCarlo Pisacane‚Äù group

Ettore Tulli was one of the central figures of the first armed Resistance in Bergamo after 8 September 1943. Coming from an anti-fascist family, Tulli early developed a clear opposition to the regime, which led him to actively engage in the struggle against fascism and the German occupation.

Immediately after the armistice, Tulli was among the protagonists of the action carried out at the Bergamo Prefecture, where he attempted to recover weapons in order to prevent them from falling into the hands of the Germans and the republican fascists. From this initiative arose one of the city‚Äôs first partisan arsenals, a fundamental element for the start of organized Resistance.

During the autumn of 1943, Tulli contributed to the formation of the partisan group ‚ÄúCarlo Pisacane,‚Äù initially active between Bergamo and the Brembana Valley, particularly in the areas of Santa Brigida and Erna. The choice of the name ‚ÄúPisacane‚Äù explicitly recalled the values of the Risorgimento and the sacrifice for freedom, emphasizing the ideal link between the partisan struggle and the battles for national unity and independence.

The Pisacane group operated in an early and difficult phase of the Resistance, characterized by a shortage of weapons, precarious organization, and harsh repression by the fascist authorities. For this very reason, the role of Tulli and his companions is particularly significant: they represented one of the first nuclei of armed opposition in the Bergamo area, anticipating forms of struggle that would later become more structured in the months that followed.
